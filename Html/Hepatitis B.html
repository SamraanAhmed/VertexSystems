Hepatitis

Overview

Introduction:

Hepatitis is liver inflammation caused by viruses A-E. As of 2025, 354 million have chronic hepatitis; vaccines for A/B key to prevention. Acute for A/E, chronic for B/C/D.

Cause:

Five main viral types: HAV, HBV, HCV, HDV, HEV; differ in transmission and chronicity.

Types of Hepatitis Viruses

A-E; B/C most chronic. 2025 progress report shows gains in vaccination.

Structure / Nature of Hepatitis Viruses

Varied: Non-enveloped RNA for HAV/HEV, enveloped DNA for HBV, enveloped RNA for HCV.

  • Genome: RNA (HAV, HCV, HEV) or DNA (HBV).
  • Surface: Non-enveloped (HAV/HEV) or enveloped (HBV/HCV).
  • Shape: Small, 27-42 nm.

Transmission

Fecal-oral (HAV, HEV)

Contaminated food/water.

Bloodborne (HBV, HCV, HDV)

Needles, transfusions, razors.

Sexual (HBV)

Unprotected sex.

Perinatal (HBV)

Mother to child at birth.

Not casual contact

No spread by hugging or food handling.

Common Symptoms

Fatigue

Jaundice

Abdominal pain

Nausea

Dark urine

Loss of appetite

Fever

Joint pain

Diseases / Complications

Acute symptoms resolve, but chronic leads to liver damage. 2025 goals reduce new infections.

Mild / Acute Complications
  • Dehydration
  • Cholestasis
  • Relapsing HAV
Severe / Chronic Complications
  • Cirrhosis
  • Liver cancer (HCC)
  • Liver failure
  • Fulminant hepatitis (rare)
High-Risk Groups

More likely to acquire or progress:

  • Children in endemic areas
  • Healthcare workers
  • Injection drug users
  • Pregnant women (HEV)
  • Chronic liver disease patients

Prevention

1
Vaccines (A, B)

Routine for infants; travel for A.

2
Safe water/food

For HAV/HEV; boil or treat water.

3
Safe sex

Condoms for HBV.

4
Clean needles

For HBV/HCV; no sharing.

5
Blood screening

Transfusions tested for HBV/HCV.

6
HBV birth dose

Vaccine within 24 hours for newborns.
