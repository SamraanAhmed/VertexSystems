Herpes (HSV)

Overview

Introduction:

Herpes simplex virus (HSV) causes oral/genital sores; lifelong latent infection. As of 2025, 50-80% US adults have oral HSV-1, 1 in 6 genital HSV-2 (14-49); 846 million global genital cases.

Cause:

HSV-1 (oral, 3.7 billion global) and HSV-2 (genital); spreads skin-to-skin. No cure, antivirals manage.

Types of Herpes Simplex Virus

Two types; both cause blisters, latent in nerves. Asymptomatic shedding common.

  • Primarily oral (cold sores).
  • 50-80% US adults infected.
  • Can cause genital via oral sex.
  • Latent in trigeminal ganglion.
  • Primarily genital herpes.
  • 1 in 6 ages 14-49 US.
  • Higher recurrence rate.
  • Latent in sacral ganglion.

Structure / Nature of HSV Virus

Enveloped double-stranded DNA virus in Herpesviridae; icosahedral capsid. Latent in sensory neurons.

  • DNA Genome: Linear double-stranded DNA (~152 kb).
  • Surface Proteins: Glycoproteins for entry/fusion.
  • Shape: Enveloped icosahedral, 150-200 nm.

Transmission

Skin-to-skin contact

During outbreaks or asymptomatic shedding.

Kissing/oral sex (HSV-1)

Mouth-to-mouth or mouth-to-genital.

Sexual contact (HSV-2)

Vaginal, anal, oral sex.

Mother-to-child

During birth if active lesions.

Not blood/airborne

No casual or environmental spread.

Common Symptoms

Blisters/sores

Itching/tingling

Pain

Fever (first outbreak)

Swollen glands

Headache

Body aches

Discharge (genital)

Diseases / Complications

Usually mild; recurrent outbreaks. Rare severe in neonates/immunocompromised.

Mild / Recurrent
  • Secondary bacterial infection
  • Emotional stress
  • Asymptomatic shedding
Severe Complications (Rare)
  • Neonatal herpes
  • Encephalitis
  • Meningitis
  • Ocular herpes
High-Risk Groups

More likely to have severe outcomes:

  • Newborns
  • Immunocompromised
  • Eye involvement
  • Pregnant women
  • Frequent recurrences

Prevention

1
Condoms/dental dams

Reduce genital transmission by 30-50%.

2
Avoid contact during outbreaks

No kissing/sex with active sores.

3
Suppressive therapy

Daily antivirals reduce shedding/transmission.

4
C-section for active genital

Prevents neonatal transmission.

5
Testing and disclosure

Know status; inform partners.

6
Limit partners

Fewer partners lowers risk.

Antiviral Medications

Acyclovir, valacyclovir, famciclovir; episodic or suppressive. Reduce duration by 1 day, shedding by 70%. 2025 research on skin penetration.

Severe Cases

IV antivirals for neonatal/encephalitis; hospitalization.

  • Topical for mild
  • Oral for outbreaks
No cure; manage recurrences.
